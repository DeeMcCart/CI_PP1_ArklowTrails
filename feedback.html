About

Arklow has a rich and varied landscape with coastal, riverside, woodland and hilly terrain.


Coastal walks offer spectacular views along the Wicklow and Wexford coastlines, particularly from elevated viewing points. This includes the coastal path at Arklow Nature reserve ('North beach'), and at Arklow Rock ('South Beach'). Experience the ever-changing mood of the Irish Sea and sky, and capture wonderful photos...


The north beach is largely eroded, however continuing the Nature Reserve walk through the caravan park leads to a small sandy beach. There is the possibility of exploring further if you are willing to climb headlands to reach small-to-medium beaches threaded along the rocky Wicklow coastline. Remains of the Kynochs gunpowder plant, dating from the 1900's, can be seen on this route.


Glenart to the southwest, and Kilbride to the northwest, are woodland trails on opposite sides of the Avoca river valley. Of historic interest are Arklow Pyramid (actually a burial crypt) and Glenart castle.

Arklow maritime museum. Walking, hiking and jogging trails have been cultivated to highlight points of interest
